Burn Classification System

Welcome to the NovaBurn ai - Skin Burn Classification System. This tool helps to assess the severity of skin burns using image analysis.

NovaBurn ai will classify the burns into the 3 burn degrees. Also it provides a heatmap of the burn to make better decisions.

First Degree Burns / Superficial Burns

First-degree burns affect only the outer layer of skin (epidermis). They cause redness, pain, and minor swelling but don't usually cause blistering.

Symptoms:

  • Redness
  • Pain
  • Minor Swelling
  • Skin Dryness
  • Peeling as the burn heals

Treatment:

  • Cool the burn with cool (not cold)
    running water for 10-15 minutes
  • Apply aloe vera or moisturizer
  • Take over-the-counter pain relievers if needed
  • Protect the area with a sterile, non-adhesive bandage

Second Degree Burns / Partial Thickness Burns

Second-degree burns affect both the epidermis and dermis layers of skin. They cause blisters, severe pain, and swelling.

Symptoms:

  • Blisters
  • Severe pain and swelling
  • Red, white or splotchy skin
  • Wet appearance due to fluid leaking
  • Possible scarring

Treatment:

  • Run cool water over the area for 15 minutes
  • Do not break blisters
  • Apply antibiotic ointment
  • Cover with a sterile, non-stick bandage
  • Seek medical attention for large or severe burns

Third Degree Burns / Full Thickness Burns

Third-degree burns extend through all layers of skin and may affect deeper tissues. They appear white or charred and may not be painful due to nerve damage.

Symptoms:

  • White, brown, or black skin
  • Charred appearance
  • Leathery texture
  • Numbness due to nerve damage
  • Severe scarring

Treatment:

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Do not remove burned clothing
  • Cover the area with a clean, dry cloth
  • Elevate the burned area above heart level if possible
  • Monitor for signs of shock
